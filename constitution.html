Computer Science Women's Network Constitution

Updated 9/15/2015

Article I

Name

The name of this organization is Computer Science Women’s Network.

Article II

Purposes

The purposes of this organization are to support members, especially to incoming freshmen women, by helping members find appropriate courses for their interests, providing information on possible jobs or where to find information for possible jobs, and creating a community for women in technology. The organization seeks to promote an environment, both within the department and society, which will increase overall performance in computer science as a whole. Special emphasis will be placed on issues dealing with women and strengthening relationships among computer scientists to better facilitate the sharing of knowledge and information.

Article III

Membership

Membership in Computer Science Women’s Network is open to any Purdue University student who is interested in women’s issues with respect to computer science and society. Associate membership is open to any Purdue University faculty or staff member and concerned members of the community. Membership and participation shall be free from discrimination based on race, religion, color, sex, age, national origin or ancestry, marital status, parental status, sexual orientation, disability, or status as a disabled or Vietnam-era veteran. There shall be no membership dues.

Article IV

Officers

The official office structure of Computer Science Women’s Network will consist of the following positions: President, Vice President, Treasurer, Secretary, Corporate Liaison, Webmaster, and Event Coordinator. Officers shall serve a term of one year. To be considered for an officer position, students must be considered a voting member and at least have a 2.0 cumulative GPA. Aspiring officers must complete an online application prior to elections. In order to fully qualify as a voting member of Computer Science Women’s Network, one must attend at least two events and three of the weekly officer meetings per semester. The officers will be chosen by an election to be held in the month of April. Elections will be held in person and any voting member can attend.

In the event of a vacancy, a new officer will be selected by the executive board. In the event that an officer ceases to be classified as a voting member, their position will become vacant.

The advisor(s) for the Computer Science Women’s Network shall be either a faculty or staff member and shall be chosen by the executive board. She or he shall serve a term of one year until the advisor resigns or is asked to resign by all officers. The advisor shall act as a liaison between the organization, the computer science department, and the university.

Article V

Officers and Duties

President:

Primary contact person. Responsible for coordinating communications and activities between all club officers. The president will call and facilitate all meetings and work with the other officers to set a meeting agenda.

Vice President:

Responsible for all of the president’s duties in the event that the president is not capable of performing them, within reason. The Vice President is also in charge of updating the glass case for the club in Lawson as well as making sure the club office is in presentable condition. This position entails making content for promotional materials if needed as well.

Treasurer:

The treasurer is in charge of creating and managing the club budget and finding new sources of income. The treasurer also keeps accounts, deposits the organization’s funds, and makes expenditures in a manner approved by the Business Office for Student Organizations.

Secretary:

The secretary is responsible for the meeting minutes for every officer meeting, as well as updating all social media associated with CSWN (Facebook, Twitter, etc.).

Webmaster

This position involves updating the CSWN website (http://www.cs.purdue.edu/cswn).

Event Coordinator:

The Event Coordinator is responsible for making sure all food and materials are ordered or bought for each event and ensures the timely arrival of these items.

Corporate Liaison:

This position involves emailing companies or prospective parties on behalf of CSWN in a timely manner. The Corporate Liaison assists with CSWN company and event sponsorships.

Article VI

Amendments

Amendments to this constitution shall be approved by a majority vote of the officers. All amendments are subject to the approval of the Office of the Dean of Students.
